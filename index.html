⚠️ Snake Safety — Port Douglas Hiking
  • Wear closed shoes + long socks/pants — poisonous snakes are present and can be aggressive
  • Stick to well-marked trails only · Carry water, insect repellent, sunscreen
  • Avoid hiking at dawn/dusk — peak snake activity times
  • Check trail conditions at visitor centres before heading out

Before You Go

📋 Still To Confirm
  • Australian ETA visas — apply online for all travelers at eta.homeaffairs.gov.au
  • Passport validity — must be valid 6+ months beyond Apr 19
  • Daintree tour operator (Apr 16) — Tony's Tropical or Back Country Bliss
  • Babel Port Douglas dinner (Apr 14)
  • Sydney Opera House guided tour — book online
  • Taronga Zoo tickets — buy online in advance
🇦🇺 Australia Essentials
  • Power: Type I plugs — bring adapters
  • Currency: AUD · Cards widely accepted
  • Tipping: Not expected, but appreciated
  • Sun: UV is extreme — SPF 50+, hats, rashguards essential
  • Reef sunscreen: Must be reef-safe at the GBR
  • Beach swim: Between the flags only — always
  • Emergency: Call 000
⏰ Practical Tips
  • Queensland & Sydney both UTC+10 in April — no time zone change within Australia
  • Notify your bank of international travel before Apr 8
  • Travel insurance recommended for all 4 travelers
  • Declare any food/biosecurity items on US customs form on return
  • Keep all confirmations in one folder — screenshot backups recommended
