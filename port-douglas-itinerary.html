    Ref: 16866285   wildlifehabitat.com.au →
  • Afternoon

    Stroll Macrossan Street, browse the Sunday markets (if running), and dip your toes in Four Mile Beach.

    Early night — reef day tomorrow!

    Dinner near the Coconut Grove. The Reef Hotel or On the Inlet are solid choices. Set alarms — early departure for the reef.

Port Douglas is the premier gateway to two UNESCO World Heritage Sites — the Great Barrier Reef and the Daintree Rainforest. Activities range from swimming near saltwater crocodiles and white-water rafting to ziplining through ancient forest canopies and drift-floating down tropical rivers.

Hiking Day

Headlands, rainforest & optional adventures

  • 6:30 AM

    Flagstaff Hill / Four Mile Headland Walk

    Start early before the heat. Short drive or walk from Coconut Grove.

    Distance: 3 km (extendable) • Difficulty: Moderate — steep uphill sections, mostly clear footing
    Highlights: Panoramic views of Four Mile Beach and Coral Sea. Good mix of forested trail and open headlands. Very safe; open trail with low snake risk.

    3 km • Moderate • Panoramic views
  • Morning

    Breakfast & rest time

    Refuel at a Macrossan Street cafe — Zinc or Mango Jam are local favourites.

  • Afternoon

    Optional Activity — Pick One:

🏊
River Drift Snorkeling

Float & snorkel down the crystal-clear Mossman River through the rainforest. Unique & memorable for kids.

🌿
Ziplining — Cape Tribulation

Treetop Adventures — soar up to 15 m above ground through the world's oldest rainforest canopy.

treetopsadventure.com.au →
🚣
White-Water Rafting

Half-day trips on the Barron River — Grade 2–3 rapids through rugged rainforest ravines. Thrilling for older kids.

🚙
4WD Off-Road Tour

Deep Daintree 4WD tours including ATV rides across creeks and hills on private properties.

⚠️ Safety Tips for Hiking Port Douglas
  • Wear closed shoes and long socks/pants to prevent snake bites — poisonous snakes can be aggressive here
  • Stick to well-marked trails only
  • Carry water, insect repellent & sunscreen at all times
  • Avoid hiking at dawn/dusk when snakes are most active
  • Check for recent trail conditions at visitor centres or local park websites before heading out

    Spot massive saltwater crocodiles sunning on the river banks, plus kingfishers and rare birds.

    The magical place where the Daintree Rainforest meets the Great Barrier Reef — one of the most iconic spots in Australia. Watch for cassowaries on the beach.

  • Afternoon
